Skulls and Roses Rules (Regular Deck Version)

Skulls and Roses is a game of bluffing, deduction, and daring. This version uses the red and black cards from a standard deck of playing cards to represent roses and skulls, respectively.

Setup

  • Each player receives 4 cards: 3 red cards (Roses) and 1 black card (Skull).
  • Players hold their cards face down and may not reveal their cards to others.
  • Each player also has a token or marker to track their victories.

Objective

Be the first player to successfully complete two challenges by flipping over a declared number of cards without revealing a Skull.

Gameplay

  • Players take turns, proceeding clockwise.
  • On your turn, place one card from your hand face down in front of you. You may add additional cards in subsequent turns.
  • Once all players have placed at least one card, players may place another card face down, or start bidding:
    • The first player to bid the number of cards they think they can flip over without revealing a Skull.
    • Other players may raise the bid or pass. If you pass, you cannot bid again this round.
    • The highest bidder attempts the challenge.

Challenges

  • The highest bidder must flip over the declared number of cards, starting with all of their own cards.
  • If they succeed (only revealing Roses), they win the challenge and gain 1 victory token.
  • If they fail (reveal a Skull), they lose one of their cards permanently, chosen randomly by the player who owned the revealed Skull.
  • Players may not reveal their remaining cards to others.

End of the Game

  • The first player to win 2 challenges wins the game.
  • If a player loses all their cards, they are eliminated.
